Day 1: Daventry → Strasbourg

Drive Time

  • Daventry to Folkestone: ~2.5–3 hours
  • Eurotunnel Crossing: ~35 minutes + check-in
  • Calais to Strasbourg: ~5 hours

Morning

  • Depart Daventry by 6:00 AM
  • Board Eurotunnel around 9:30–10:00 AM

Lunch Stop

  • Reims – Enjoy classic French cuisine at Brasserie du Boulingrin

Arrival

  • Reach Strasbourg by 5:00–6:00 PM
  • Check-in at Hotel Cour du Corbeau Strasbourg MGallery
Hotel: Hotel Cour du Corbeau Strasbourg MGallery
A historic 4-star boutique hotel near Petite France, offering luxurious accommodations and exceptional service.

Evening in Strasbourg

  • Stroll through the picturesque Petite France District
  • Dine at Le Clou or Chez Yvonne, savoring traditional Alsatian dishes like tarte flambée

Day 2: Exploring Strasbourg

Sights & Activities

  • Strasbourg Cathedral: Climb to the tower for panoramic city views
  • Batorama Boat Tour: Glide along the River Ill, enjoying scenic vistas
  • Orangerie Park: Perfect for a leisurely morning stroll amidst beautiful gardens
  • European Parliament: Admire modern architecture and the significance of this landmark

Lunch

  • Au Pont Corbeau – Delight in an authentic Alsatian ambiance with exquisite local dishes

Afternoon

  • Visit local markets to browse fresh produce, cheeses, and artisanal goods
  • Optional museum visit: Musée Alsacien – Discover regional folklore and traditional furnishings

Evening

  • Indulge in a local wine tasting session, featuring Alsatian Riesling and Gewürztraminer
  • Dine at Chez Yvonne or Le Clou, enjoying refined Alsatian cuisine
Hotel: Continue your stay at Hotel Cour du Corbeau Strasbourg MGallery, perfectly situated for evening strolls and dining.

Day 3: Strasbourg → Black Forest

Drive Time

  • Strasbourg to Baden-Baden: ~1 hour
  • Afternoon Scenic Drive: Schwarzwaldhochstraße (B500)

Morning in Baden-Baden

  • Depart Strasbourg by 9:00 AM
  • Relax at Caracalla Spa or Friedrichsbad, renowned for their therapeutic thermal baths

Lunch

  • Dine at Brenners Park-Hotel Bistro, offering an upscale meal with local produce

Afternoon Scenic Drive

  • Continue along the Schwarzwaldhochstraße (B500), one of Germany's most scenic routes
  • Stop at Mummelsee – Enjoy a short lakeside walk and browse for unique Black Forest souvenirs
Hotel Options:
1) Brenners Park-Hotel & Spa in Baden-Baden – Iconic luxury with extensive spa facilities.
2) Hotel Dollenberg in Bad Peterstal-Griesbach – Secluded wellness retreat with panoramic views.

Evening

  • Experience Michelin-starred dining at Brenners Park-Restaurant for a refined culinary experience
  • Alternatively, enjoy gourmet cuisine at Restaurant Bareiss in Baiersbronn

Day 4: Black Forest Exploration

Drive Times

  • Baden-Baden to Triberg: ~1.5 hours
  • Triberg to Titisee: ~1 hour

Morning

  • Visit the breathtaking Triberg Waterfalls, Germany’s highest accessible falls
  • Explore the Eble Uhren-Park – Home to the world’s largest cuckoo clock

Lunch

  • Dine at Bergseestüble, known for its hearty Black Forest specialties

Afternoon

  • Drive to Lake Titisee for a relaxing afternoon
  • Options include a pedal boat ride, lakeside cafés, or sampling the famous Black Forest cake
Hotel: Boutique Hotel Alemannenhof
A cozy lakeside property near Titisee, offering picturesque views and exceptional comfort.

Evening

  • Dine at the hotel’s restaurant or explore local eateries in Titisee-Neustadt, savoring Black Forest trout

Day 5: Black Forest → Avignon

Drive Time

  • ~6.5–7 hours

Possible Stops

  • Basel, Switzerland – Quick city and Rhine River views
  • Lyon, France – Renowned for its gastronomic delights

Lunch

  • If stopping in Lyon: Enjoy traditional Lyonnaise cuisine at Le Bouchon des Cordeliers

Arrival

  • Reach Avignon by 4:00–5:00 PM
  • Check-in at La Mirande, a historic luxury hotel near Palais des Papes
Hotel: La Mirande
Timeless Provençal elegance with an acclaimed dining experience, steps away from Avignon’s top sights.

Evening

  • Tour the magnificent Palais des Papes (check closing times)
  • Stroll across the iconic Pont d’Avignon, perfect for sunset photos
  • Dine at La Mirande’s restaurant or a nearby Provençal bistro, enjoying regional specialties

Day 6: Avignon → Jávea

Drive Time

  • ~7 hours

Possible Stops

  • Montpellier or Narbonne – Quick coffee or stretch break
  • Girona, Spain (~3.5 hrs from Avignon) – Experience medieval charm and fantastic restaurants

Lunch

  • In Girona: Savor top-rated local fare at Bionbo Café Gastrobar or Le Bistrot

Arrival

  • Arrive by late afternoon (4:00–5:00 PM)
Hotel: Parador de Jávea
A classic Spanish coastal retreat with lush gardens and stunning sea views, offering luxurious accommodations.

Evening

  • Take a leisurely walk along Arenal Beach, enjoying the sunset and vibrant beachside atmosphere
  • Dine at La Perla de Jávea or Restaurante Tosca, known for exquisite seafood and Mediterranean specialties

Optional Days 7–8: Leisure in Jávea

  • Montgó Natural Park: Enjoy gentle hiking trails with panoramic sea and mountain vistas
  • Old Town Jávea: Explore historic streets, visit charming churches, and indulge in tapas at local bars
  • Nearby day trips to Moraira or Denia for additional beaches and waterfront dining

Accommodation Tips:

Extend your stay at Parador de Jávea for continued luxury or consider the Nomad Hotel (a boutique property with a chic rooftop bar) for a stylish change of scenery.
