Day 1: Daventry to Strasbourg

Drive Time

  • Daventry to Folkestone: ~2.5–3 hours
  • Eurotunnel Crossing: ~35 minutes + check-in
  • Calais to Strasbourg: ~5 hours

Morning

  • Depart Daventry by 6:00 AM
  • Eurotunnel ~9:30–10:00 AM

Lunch Stop

  • Reims – Try Brasserie du Boulingrin for classic French cuisine

Arrival

  • Arrive in Strasbourg by 5:00–6:00 PM
  • Check-in at Hotel Cour du Corbeau Strasbourg MGallery
Hotel: Hotel Cour du Corbeau Strasbourg MGallery
A historic 4-star boutique hotel near Petite France

Evening in Strasbourg

  • Explore Petite France District
  • Enjoy Alsatian dinner at Le Clou or Chez Yvonne

Day 2: Full Day in Strasbourg

Sights & Activities

  • Strasbourg Cathedral – Climb the tower for panoramic views
  • Batorama Boat Tour – Scenic canal ride
  • Orangerie Park – Relaxing green space
  • European Parliament area – Modern architectural contrast

Dining

  • Lunch: Au Pont Corbeau (authentic Alsatian ambiance)
  • Dinner: Enjoy local wines (Riesling, Gewürztraminer) and dine at Chez Yvonne or Le Clou
Hotel: Continue at Hotel Cour du Corbeau
Perfectly situated for sightseeing and dining.

Day 3: Strasbourg to the Black Forest (Baden-Baden & Beyond)

Drive Time

  • Strasbourg to Baden-Baden: ~1 hour
  • Afternoon scenic drive on the Schwarzwaldhochstraße (B500)

Morning

  • Depart by 9:00 AM
  • Baden-Baden: Caracalla Spa or Friedrichsbad

Lunch

  • Consider Brenners Park-Hotel bistro for an upscale meal

Afternoon Scenic Route

  • Drive along the B500
  • Stop at Mummelsee – short lake walk, souvenir shopping
Hotel Options:
1) Brenners Park-Hotel & Spa in Baden-Baden (iconic luxury)
2) Hotel Dollenberg if you prefer deeper Black Forest serenity

Evening

  • Michelin-starred dining at Brenners Park-Restaurant
  • Enjoy the hotel spa or stroll local trails

Day 4: Black Forest Exploration (Triberg & Titisee)

Drive Times

  • Baden-Baden to Triberg: ~1.5 hours
  • Triberg to Titisee: ~1 hour

Morning

  • Triberg Waterfalls – Germany’s highest accessible falls
  • World’s largest cuckoo clock at Eble Uhren-Park

Lunch in Triberg

  • Bergseestüble – Hearty Black Forest specialties

Afternoon

  • Drive to Lake Titisee
  • Relax lakeside, rent a pedal boat, or sample Black Forest cake
Hotel: Boutique Hotel Alemannenhof
Cozy lakeside property near Titisee with excellent views.

Evening

  • Dinner at the hotel restaurant or local eateries in Titisee-Neustadt

Day 5: Black Forest to Avignon

Drive Time

  • ~6.5–7 hours

Possible Stops

  • Basel (Switzerland) for a quick city & Rhine break
  • Lyon (France) for gastronomic delights

Lunch

  • If via Lyon: Le Bouchon des Cordeliers for traditional Lyonnaise cuisine

Arrival in Avignon

  • Reach by ~4:00–5:00 PM
  • Check-in at La Mirande, a historic luxury hotel near Palais des Papes
Hotel: La Mirande
Refined Provençal style, renowned dining, steps from Avignon’s top sights.

Evening

  • Tour the Palais des Papes (check closing times)
  • Walk across the Pont d’Avignon
  • Dinner in the hotel’s restaurant or a nearby Provençal bistro

Day 6: Avignon to Jávea

Drive Time

  • ~7 hours

Possible Stops

  • Montpellier or Narbonne – Quick coffee breaks
  • Girona (3.5 hrs from Avignon) – Medieval charm and fantastic restaurants

Lunch

  • Bionbo Café Gastrobar or Le Bistrot in Girona for top-rated local fare

Arrival in Jávea

  • Arrive by late afternoon (4:00–5:00 PM)
Hotel: Parador de Jávea
A classic Spanish coastal property with lush gardens and sea views.

Evening

  • Stroll along Arenal Beach
  • Dine at La Perla de Jávea or Restaurante Tosca for exquisite seafood

Optional Day 7–8 in Jávea

  • Montgó Natural Park: Hike with stunning coastal panoramas.
  • Old Town Jávea: Explore medieval streets, markets, and charming tapas bars.
  • Nearby day trips to Moraira or Denia for additional beaches and waterfront dining.

Note:

Extend your stay at Parador de Jávea or consider the Nomad Hotel (boutique property with a rooftop bar) if you prefer a change of scenery.
